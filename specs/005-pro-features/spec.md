# Feature Specification: Pro Features Pack

**Feature Branch**: `005-pro-features`
**Created**: 2026-06-19
**Status**: Draft

## Context

MailMind AI currently offers a Free plan (10 emails/month, 3 clients) and a Pro plan ($9/mo, unlimited). The Pro plan now has billing infrastructure (Lemon Squeezy) but no exclusive features beyond lifted limits. This pack gives Pro users tangible extra value to justify the upgrade and reduce churn.

Six capabilities are added exclusively for Pro users:
1. **Email Templates** — save reusable email bodies
2. **Advanced Tones** — expand from 3 tones to 6 (add Urgent, Apologetic, Persuasive)
3. **Client Notes** — private notes attached to each client record
4. **CSV Export** — one-click download of email history and client list
5. **Email Signature Editor** — create a personalised closing signature appended to every sent email
6. **Priority AI Model** — Pro users' emails are generated by a more capable AI model

---

## User Scenarios & Testing *(mandatory)*

### User Story 1 — Save and Reuse an Email Template (Priority: P1)

A Pro freelancer often sends the same payment reminder each month with minor edits. She composes the email once in MailMind AI, saves it as a template called "Monthly Invoice Follow-up", and next month opens the template, tweaks two lines, and sends — instead of re-describing the context to the AI.

**Why this priority**: Templates are the highest-ROI Pro feature; they remove repetitive AI calls and save time on every repeat communication.

**Independent Test**: Create a template, navigate away, open it again, verify content is intact, use it in a new compose session.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a Pro user is on the Compose page, **When** they click "Save as Template" after generating an email, **Then** the subject and body are saved under a user-chosen template name.
2. **Given** saved templates exist, **When** the user opens the template picker in Compose, **Then** all their templates are listed with name and preview.
3. **Given** the user selects a template, **When** it loads into Compose, **Then** the subject and body fields are pre-filled and editable.
4. **Given** a template exists, **When** the user deletes it, **Then** it is permanently removed and no longer appears in the picker.
5. **Given** a Free user, **When** they try to access templates, **Then** they see an upgrade prompt — templates are not accessible.

---

### User Story 2 — Email Signature Editor (Priority: P2)

A Pro consultant wants every email to close with his name, title, website, and phone number. He writes his signature once in Profile settings. From then on, every email sent through MailMind AI automatically appends his signature beneath the AI-generated body.

**Why this priority**: Signature is a one-time setup with ongoing value on every email — minimal implementation effort, high perceived professionalism.

**Independent Test**: Set signature in Profile, send one email, verify signature appears in the sent message.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a Pro user is on the Profile page, **When** they enter text in the signature editor and save, **Then** the signature is persisted to their account.
2. **Given** a saved signature exists, **When** a Pro user sends or schedules an email, **Then** the signature is appended below the AI-generated body with a blank line separator.
3. **Given** a Pro user clears the signature field and saves, **When** they send the next email, **Then** no signature is appended.
4. **Given** a Free user on the Profile page, **When** they view the signature section, **Then** they see a read-only notice that signature requires Pro.

---

### User Story 3 — Advanced Tone Selection (Priority: P2)

A Pro user needs to send an overdue-payment email. "Strict" isn't quite right — he wants "Urgent". He selects the Urgent tone, and the AI generates copy that conveys deadline pressure without being rude.

**Why this priority**: Tones are already integrated into the AI pipeline; adding 3 more tones is low-effort and makes Pro feel noticeably richer.

**Independent Test**: Select each new tone in Compose, generate an email, verify the AI output reflects the chosen tone.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a Pro user is on the Compose page, **When** they open the tone selector, **Then** they see 6 options: Friendly, Formal, Strict, Urgent, Apologetic, Persuasive.
2. **Given** a Free user on Compose, **When** they open the tone selector, **Then** they see only 3 options (Friendly, Formal, Strict) and a lock icon or note for the other 3.
3. **Given** a Pro user selects "Urgent", **When** they generate an email, **Then** the output uses deadline-focused, time-sensitive language.
4. **Given** a Pro user selects "Apologetic", **When** they generate an email, **Then** the output acknowledges a mistake or delay with empathy.
5. **Given** a Pro user selects "Persuasive", **When** they generate an email, **Then** the output uses benefit-led, motivating language.

---

### User Story 4 — Client Notes (Priority: P3)

A Pro freelancer wants to remember that a client always wants invoices before the 25th and prefers formal language. She opens the client's profile, writes a private note, and can refer to it before composing future emails.

**Why this priority**: Notes increase the depth of the client management feature and create stickiness.

**Independent Test**: Add a note to a client, navigate away, open the client record, verify note is visible.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a Pro user is on the Client detail page, **When** they click "Add Note", type text, and save, **Then** the note appears below the client info.
2. **Given** notes exist on a client, **When** the Pro user views the client, **Then** all notes are shown in reverse-chronological order with timestamps.
3. **Given** a note exists, **When** the Pro user deletes it, **Then** it is permanently removed.
4. **Given** a Free user on the Client detail page, **When** they view the notes section, **Then** it shows an upgrade prompt instead of note input.

---

### User Story 5 — CSV Export (Priority: P3)

A Pro user wants to give their accountant a spreadsheet of all invoices sent this month. They click "Export Emails" on the dashboard and receive a CSV file with date, client, subject, status, and type columns.

**Why this priority**: CSV export is a standard data-ownership feature; it differentiates Pro from Free and is low complexity.

**Independent Test**: Click Export on the dashboard with at least one sent email; verify a valid CSV file downloads with correct headers and data.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a Pro user on the Dashboard, **When** they click "Export Emails", **Then** a CSV file downloads immediately with columns: Date, Client Name, Client Email, Subject, Status, Email Type.
2. **Given** a Pro user on the Clients page, **When** they click "Export Clients", **Then** a CSV file downloads with columns: Name, Email, Phone, Company, Address, Added Date.
3. **Given** a Free user, **When** they see the export button (if shown), **Then** clicking it shows an upgrade prompt.
4. **Given** a Pro user with zero emails, **When** they export, **Then** they receive a valid CSV with headers only and no data rows.

---

### User Story 6 — Priority AI Model for Pro (Priority: P4)

A Pro user notices their generated emails are more nuanced and better structured than before upgrading. Under the hood, the system routes Pro users to a higher-capability AI model (e.g., Gemini 2.5 Pro or Claude) while Free users use the standard free-tier model.

**Why this priority**: The UX difference is invisible to users but adds real value when they notice quality improvements; it also justifies the price difference to technically-minded users.

**Independent Test**: Generate an email as a Free user (check model used in response), then as a Pro user, verify a different model ID is returned.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a Pro user generates an email, **When** the AI response is returned, **Then** the `model_used` field reflects a premium model (e.g., `gemini-2.5-pro` or `claude-haiku-4-5`).
2. **Given** a Free user generates an email, **When** the AI response is returned, **Then** the `model_used` field reflects the standard model (`gemini-flash`).
3. **Given** the Pro model is unavailable, **When** the system falls back, **Then** it falls back to the standard model without exposing an error to the user.

---

### Edge Cases

- Template name collision: Two templates with the same name are allowed (names are not unique keys).
- Signature with HTML/markdown: Signature is stored and sent as plain text; no HTML rendering.
- CSV with special characters: Client names or subjects containing commas or quotes are properly escaped per RFC 4180.
- Note length: Notes are capped at 2,000 characters per note.
- Template body length: Templates are capped at 10,000 characters (same as email body limit).
- Downgrade to Free: Templates, notes, and signature remain stored but are inaccessible until re-upgrade; data is not deleted.

## Requirements *(mandatory)*

### Functional Requirements

**Email Templates**
- **FR-001**: Pro users MUST be able to save the current compose subject and body as a named template.
- **FR-002**: Pro users MUST be able to view, load, and delete their saved templates from within the Compose flow.
- **FR-003**: System MUST enforce a cap of 50 templates per user to prevent abuse.
- **FR-004**: Free users MUST see an upgrade prompt when attempting to access templates.

**Email Signature**
- **FR-005**: Pro users MUST be able to enter and save a plain-text email signature (max 500 characters) on the Profile page.
- **FR-006**: System MUST automatically append the saved signature to every outgoing email body, separated by a blank line.
- **FR-007**: Free users MUST see the signature field as locked with an upgrade prompt.

**Advanced Tones**
- **FR-008**: Pro users MUST have access to three additional tone options in Compose: Urgent, Apologetic, Persuasive.
- **FR-009**: The AI generation prompt for each new tone MUST produce distinctly different output compared to the three standard tones.
- **FR-010**: Free users MUST see the three additional tones as locked/disabled with a visual indicator.

**Client Notes**
- **FR-011**: Pro users MUST be able to add, view, and delete plain-text notes on any client record.
- **FR-012**: Notes MUST display in reverse-chronological order with creation timestamps.
- **FR-013**: Notes MUST be capped at 2,000 characters each.
- **FR-014**: Free users MUST see a locked notes section with an upgrade prompt.

**CSV Export**
- **FR-015**: Pro users MUST be able to download their full email history as a CSV from the Dashboard.
- **FR-016**: Pro users MUST be able to download their full client list as a CSV from the Clients page.
- **FR-017**: Exported CSVs MUST follow RFC 4180 formatting (quoted fields, UTF-8 encoding).
- **FR-018**: Free users MUST see an upgrade prompt when attempting to export.

**Priority AI Model**
- **FR-019**: When a Pro user generates an email, the system MUST route the request to the configured premium AI model.
- **FR-020**: When the premium model is unavailable, the system MUST fall back to the standard model without user-visible errors.
- **FR-021**: The `model_used` field in the AI response MUST reflect the actual model that generated the email.

### Key Entities

- **EmailTemplate**: Belongs to a user. Has name (≤100 chars), subject (≤200 chars), body (≤10,000 chars), created_at. Pro-only.
- **ClientNote**: Belongs to a client (and transitively to a user). Has body (≤2,000 chars), created_at. Pro-only.
- **EmailSignature**: Single text field on the User record (already exists as `email_signature`). Max 500 chars.

## Success Criteria *(mandatory)*

### Measurable Outcomes

- **SC-001**: Pro users can save and reload a template in under 30 seconds from the Compose page.
- **SC-002**: Pro users can add a client note in under 15 seconds from the Client page.
- **SC-003**: A CSV export of up to 500 emails completes and downloads in under 5 seconds.
- **SC-004**: All 6 tone options produce visibly distinct AI output in blind comparison.
- **SC-005**: Signature appears correctly in 100% of sent emails when set.
- **SC-006**: Free users see an upgrade prompt (not an error) for every gated feature.
- **SC-007**: Zero data loss on plan downgrade — stored templates and notes survive and re-appear on re-upgrade.

---

## Assumptions

- `email_signature` column already exists on the `users` table (confirmed in migration 001).
- The OpenRouter key supports both the standard free model and the premium model (to be configured via `OPENROUTER_PRO_MODEL` env var).
- Client notes are user-private (not shared between team members even in future Business plan).
- Templates are user-private (not shareable).
- No rich text / HTML in notes or signature — plain text only for this version.
- The Compose page already has a tone selector with Friendly, Formal, Strict; the new tones extend that UI.

---

## Out of Scope

- Shared / team templates
- Template variables (e.g., `{{client_name}}`)
- Rich-text / HTML email signature
- AI-powered note suggestions
- Email open/click tracking
- Bulk email sending
